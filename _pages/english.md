## About me

I am a PhD candidate in **Environmental Sciences and Humanities** at the University of Rennes (Géosciences Rennes × ESO Rennes laboratories), funded by a CNRS doctoral contract.

My research investigates **how 15–25-year-olds connect — or disconnect — with the living world in a digital age**, at the crossroads of environmental sociology, environmental psychology and sensory ecology. The project (*Cap Nature*) combines a quantitative survey, a PRISMA systematic review of technology-mediated nature experiences, and outdoor experimental workshops using participatory environmental sensors (bioacoustics, species-identification apps).

The central hypothesis: when well designed, these tools do not replace nature with a screen — they become a **threshold to it**. They reveal what eye and ear can no longer capture alone, and support a sensitive, reflective bond with the living.
